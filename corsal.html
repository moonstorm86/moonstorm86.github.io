Overview

Problem

There are approximately 7000 languages spoken on the planet, 40% of which face extinction within the next century. Language archives are the main means of language preservation in the face of diaspora. Issues that range from budget deficits to lack of user engagement to the proliferation of digital technology present challenges as well.

Solution

Our project sought to create a more seamless experience for linguists, members of at-risk language communities and computational linguists to transmit, store and retrieve data collected with the hopes of preserving the rich cultural heritage embedded in the language.

Research

Context

Online language archives provide the promise of long-term preservation of linguistic materials and facilitate access to these resources. The information stored may encompass diverse formats, like recordings, manuscripts, and field notes.

These items may also contain annotations that can be complex, but only for the academic user groups. In some cases, such materials provide the only surviving record of dormant languages. Archival resources also document broader cultural information, including traditional knowledge.

Contextual Research

To better understand the problem space, we conducted the following activities:

  • primary research review
  • a market analysis
  • Anxieties, Ambitions, Attitudes and Activities framework
  • systems mapping
  • research linguist journey map
  • interview with a computational linguist
    • .



Our main findings include:

  • Differnt users require differnt levels of access.
  • Just because someone is a linguist does not mean they have similar jobs; in many cases they have completely different workflows and use cases for using language archvies.
  • There is no language data base designed for the language communities that would most benefit from them.
  • Most language archives exist for the solely for academic purposes, and they typically function a lot like university libraries.

The results motivated us to:

  • Focus on researchers and field lingusits since they spend the most time in the field and already use these archives more than any user group since one of the main problems was actually getting data into these platforms.
  • Make a highly visual interface that resolves the problems of the other archives and painpoints we identified.
