Description

There are approximately 7000 languages spoken on the planet, 40% of which face extinction within the next century. Language archives are the main means of language preservation in the face of diaspora. Issues that range from budget deficits to lack of user engagement to the proliferation of digital technology present challenges as well.

Our effort sought to create a more seamless experience for linguists, members of at-risk language communities and computational linguists to transmit, store and retrieve data collected with the hopes of preserving the rich cultural heritage embedded in the language.

Online language archives provide the promise of long-term preservation of linguistic materials and facilitate access to these resources. The information stored may encompass diverse formats, like recordings, manuscripts, and field notes. These items may also contain annotations that can be complex, but only for the academic user groups. In some cases, such materials provide the only surviving record of dormant languages. Archival resources also document broader cultural information, including traditional knowledge.
