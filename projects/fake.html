Analyzing the roots of social media.

Description

Society has only recently recognized social media’s nascent power to host and amplify powerhouses’ often deceptive agendas. This real conspiracy has drawn unprecedented attention because of the 2016 election, but has roots in the newly leveraged tactic of invading the formerly native territory of social media platforms with content designed to manipulate human behavior. The capacity of these websites to louden messages is being used in increasingly nefarious ways – trackers can use the behavioral insights gleaned from mass-monitoring voluntary users of social media to form sophisticated profiles of specific segments of the population.

This method of understanding and categorizing humans on a large scale allows for hyper targeting advertisements and sponsored content so tailored that consumers often don’t realize they are being manipulated. The implementation of social media to aggregate data and use that data collected to sway people’s behavior – from spending to voting – is most problematic because of its ability to blend with truth. Fake news, branded articles, paid product integrations and more need to be taken with a grain of salt, but are often regarded as genuine. This paper briefly addresses the possibility of manipulation through the lens of fake news and politics.

Looking closer at the development of media channels reveals the complexity of change to information systems over time.

1

Starting in the 90s, when information was being consumed mainly through broadcast television and print, there began to be an increase in internet activity. In the 2000s print media began to slide even further and the internet picked up its pace allowing web browsers to function more reliably. At the end of the first decade of the 2000s we saw an explosion in mobile technology with the iphone, internet speeds yet again increased and tracking became more sophisticated. Information consumption moved largely online after this point and developed into our current model for media consumption. Within this time, social media companies like Facebook allowed for normal people to have the outreach that would only be available to large news companies in times past.

Media has changed from a uni-directional model with producers of content mostly on one end to a multifaceted channel that allows users to publish their own content with ease. Regulation was simpler when everything being produced was coming from very specific sources.
