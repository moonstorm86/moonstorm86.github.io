saffi foods

User Research | Prototyping | Product Design

Team: Jeshwanth, Sahana, Chris


Saffi Foods is a high quality olive oil distributor that was looking to develop a product system that could be used to transport bulk olive oil from grocery to pantry with an emphasis on sustainability.

00

Overview

Design Goals

Saffi’s goal is to sell olive oil in the grocery store bulk food section with a refillable container. The design should attract consumers through values based on sustainability, fulfill user requirements in the kitchen and be transportable between home and the grocery store.

User Research

James + Tomoko

James (add picture)

Design issues: Cleaning bottles is annoying Interested in having a bottle that is easier to fill Transferring bulk products into handheld packaging is appealing Bottling use cases do not translate across use scenarios

What is the use case of the oil I am buying? Buys cheaper oils in larger quantities. Cheaper oils used for more practical use cases. Transfers bulk products into a 750ml liquor bottle because it has a good handhold and is designed with hand pouring in mind. Uses speed pour spouts similar to bartenders for both cooking and pouring directly onto food.

Tomoko (add picture)

Design issues: Dripping spout Leaves oil ring When pouring olive oil, it drips down the side Possibility of bottle breaking

How long is it sitting in the bulk container? Would buy in bulk if she gets to try a smaller sample first. How long does the product stay good? What is the difference between harvest date and the date the oil was squeezed? Nuts and olive oil are analogous industry wise

Insights:

Premium containers are found to be heavier and taller in design. Design has to require minimal effort in using and cleaning. Olive oil bottles are often placed on the kitchen counter, the design needs to be elegant enough to display.
Design requirements: Airtight
Watertight
Drip proof
Easy to pour
Dishwasher safe
Refillable
Fun to use and fits Saffi brand
